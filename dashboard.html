Our Constitution

"The Constitution of India is the supreme law of the land, serving as the cornerstone of the nation's democratic framework. Adopted on November 26, 1949, and coming into effect on January 26, 1950, it outlines the fundamental rights, duties, and guiding principles for governance. This website is a comprehensive resource dedicated to providing detailed insights into the Constitution's articles, amendments, schedules, and more. Explore the essence of justice, equality, liberty, and fraternity enshrined in the Preamble, and understand how this living document continues to shape India's identity as a sovereign, socialist, secular, and democratic republic."

Founder of Constitution

Dr. B.R. Ambedkar:

Chairman of the Drafting Committee, known as the "Father of the Indian Constitution."

Jawaharlal Nehru:

First Prime Minister of India, key contributor to the vision and objectives.

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel:

Played a crucial role in unifying India and contributed to constitutional debates.

Rajendra Prasad:

President of the Constituent Assembly and the first President of India.

Alladi Krishnaswamy Iyer:

A leading member of the Drafting Committee, known for his legal acumen.

H.C. Mookherjee:

Vice President of the Constituent Assembly, contributed to the secular framework.
